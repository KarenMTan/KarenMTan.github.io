About 

I'm currently a student at UCLA. I'm pursuing a BS in Computer Science and plan to graduate in 2022.

To me, computer science is a beautiful tool used to facilitate. To expedite. To automate.

I've grown up in an age where a phone app warns me of traffic conditions on the way home. An age where I can talk to a friend continents away in under a second. An age where thousands of emails can be sorted with a click of a button.

This summer, I had the opportunity to design and develop some of my own such applications. As a co-op at Bendix Commercial Vehicle Systems' Research and Development Center in Irvine, California, I applied Python's Tkinter and pySerial libraries to create an in-house tool that helps engineers experiment with hundreds of camera registers simultaneously.

During the development process, I found significant support online. Regardless of the many "how to...with tkinter" entered or countless error messages pasted into search bars - someone had already asked. And remarkably - someone else had answered it. It reinforced my admiration and appreciation for the open community. It's one of the reasons I love this field: there are millions of people eager to learn and a million others excited to teach.

In fact, this community inspired me to join SWE@UCLA's Technical Committee where I organized and directed the 7-week Build Your Own Website Portfolio Workshop Series to teach and encourage web development to students of all majors.

I'm always excited to work on new projects! Reach out to me at karenmtan@g.ucla.edu
